Our Sacred History

Tracing the footsteps of faith, resilience, and community from the 6th century to the present day.

Comprehensive Chronological History

6th Century AD

Origins

Mar Thoma Christians settle in Thumpoly and build a small thatched church named Thomaa Pally.

AD 820

Ecclesiastical Visit

Syrian bishops Mar Sabor and Mar Proth visit and erect the Muthappan Cross.

AD 1411

Conflict & Migration

Following attacks by Elayidathu Kaimal's forces, the original church and cross are damaged; many Christians migrate to nearby Kattoor.

Royal Petitions

Manuel De Silva petitions King Veera Kerala Varma I for a permanent structure. In 1569, King Kesava Rama Varma grants permission to build where at least 30 Christian families reside.

1585

Confraternity

Jesuit priest Fr. Giacomo Fenicio establishes the first Christian Confraternity (Kombreria Darshan Samuh) in Kerala at Thumpoly.

1590 â€“ 1599

Transition

Permission is granted to replace the old church with stone and mortar (1590). The Thumpoly Perunnal festival is officially inaugurated (1599).

Construction Start

Stone construction officially begins with royal pomp in the presence of the King of Cochin.

1607

Royal Peace Offering

The King of Moothedathu donates the 'Salve Roop' (ivory statue) to the church as a sign of peace.

1730

Dedication

The current Indo-Portuguese Baroque structure is completed and dedicated in a grand ceremony attended by the King of Cochin.

2009

Heritage Restoration

A full renovation is completed using traditional materials like surkhi (lime and fish paste) to preserve the original 1730 design.
